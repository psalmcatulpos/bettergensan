# Join Disaster Drills & Evacuation Planning — General Santos City

How residents, schools, offices, and barangay councils in General Santos City can participate in **earthquake drills, fire drills, and evacuation planning** coordinated by the City Disaster Risk Reduction & Management Office (CDRRMO).

> **Coordinating office:** CDRRMO — **[552-3939](tel:+6383552393900)** · **[552-8861](tel:+6383552886100)** · mobile **[0943-461-4548](tel:+639434614548)**.

---

## National Simultaneous Earthquake Drill (NSED)

The NDRRMC and OCD run a **quarterly nationwide earthquake drill**, typically on the second Thursday of March, June, September, and November. GenSan participates city-wide.

### How to join

- **As a household:** No registration needed. When the drill bell sounds (announced on PAGASA / NDRRMC channels and local radio), practice **Drop–Cover–Hold On** for one minute, then assemble at your designated meeting point.
- **As a school or office:** Coordinate with **CDRRMO** at least 2 weeks before the date. They provide drill scenarios, evaluator checklists, and post-drill debrief support.
- **As a barangay:** The Barangay Disaster Risk Reduction & Management Committee (BDRRMC) leads the local rollout in coordination with CDRRMO.

### What's evaluated

1. Speed to reach safe positions (under 60 seconds for Drop–Cover–Hold).
2. Evacuation flow — clear stairwells, no elevator use, accounted headcount.
3. First-aid response readiness.
4. Communication chain — who calls CDRRMO, BFP, hospitals.

## Fire drills

Required annually for buildings under the Fire Code of the Philippines (RA 9514). **BFP GenSan** ([552-1160](tel:+6383552116000)) inspects and issues the **Fire Safety Evaluation Clearance (FSEC)** which depends, among other things, on completion of a documented fire drill.

### What a fire drill should include

- Activation of the fire alarm system at an announced time.
- Evacuation of the entire building (residents, staff, visitors) via designated fire exits — **not elevators**.
- Headcount at the assembly area.
- Notification drill: call **BFP `160`** (with a clear note that it is a drill).
- Post-drill review: log the time to full evacuation and any blockages or confusion.

### Frequency

- **Schools and government buildings:** at least once per semester.
- **Commercial buildings, malls, hotels, factories:** at least once per quarter.
- **Hospitals and high-occupancy assembly buildings:** monthly walk-through, quarterly full drill.

## Family / household evacuation planning

You do not need official coordination to draft your own household plan — but you should **share it with your barangay's BDRRMC** so they can include you in their head-count and special-needs registry.

### The four basics

1. **Two ways out of every room.** Identify the primary and secondary exit.
2. **A meeting point outside the house** — a known neighbor's gate, a specific tree, a barangay landmark.
3. **An out-of-area contact.** During disasters, local calls fail before long-distance ones. Pick a relative outside Mindanao who can relay messages.
4. **A go-bag per person** — see the checklist on the **[Hotlines & Early Warning page](/services/disaster-preparedness/access-disaster-preparedness-information-and-early-warning-systems)**.

### Practice it

Run the plan from the kitchen, then from the bedroom at night, then again with the lights off. Time each run. Most households shave 30–50% off their evacuation time after the third practice.

## Community-level training offered by CDRRMO

CDRRMO conducts free trainings — schedule via **[552-3939](tel:+6383552393900)** or email through the city government portal.

| Training | Audience | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Basic Life Support / CPR** | Anyone 16+ | Half-day |
| **Standard First Aid** | Anyone 16+ | 2 days |
| **Community-Based DRR for Barangay Officials** | BDRRMC members, barangay councilors | 3 days |
| **Incident Command System (ICS) for Responders** | Government employees, NGOs | 5 days |
| **Search & Rescue (basic)** | Volunteers, barangay tanod | 3 days |
| **Water Search & Rescue** | Coastal barangay teams | 5 days |

## Volunteer with the city's emergency network

- **Barangay DRR Committee (BDRRMC)** — Every barangay has one. Volunteer through your barangay hall.
- **Red Cross 143** — The Philippine Red Cross General Santos chapter trains and deploys volunteer first responders. Call **[143](tel:143)** or visit the chapter office.
- **Delta 9 (City Radio Communication Services)** — Amateur radio operators who maintain GenSan's emergency comms net. **[554-0474](tel:+6383554047400)**.
- **Boy/Girl Scouts of the Philippines** — School-based DRR auxiliaries during city-wide drills.

---

### Why this matters

In a major event, **professional responders are outnumbered by victims for the first 48 hours**. Most lives are saved by trained neighbors, not by ambulances. The point of drills is not to memorize a checklist — it's to make the right movement automatic when adrenaline takes over.
