# Emergency Hotlines & Early Warning Systems — General Santos City

Single-page reference for **who to call when something goes wrong** in General Santos City, plus the official **early-warning channels** to subscribe to before, during, and after a disaster. Use the buttons above to jump to a section.

---

## First-call dispatch

> **Life-threatening emergency? Call `911` first.** It is the 24/7 nationwide dispatch and will relay to the correct local responder.

| Service | Number | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Nationwide Emergency** | **[911](tel:911)** | Police, fire, medical — 24/7 |
| **Philippine National Police hotline** | **[117](tel:117)** | National PNP hotline |
| **Bureau of Fire Protection — Direct** | **[160](tel:160)** | GenSan BFP fire hotline |
| **Philippine Coast Guard** | **[143](tel:143)** | Sea, port, oil-spill incidents |

## Before a disaster — quick prep checklist

- **Save the numbers above** to your phone — at minimum **911**, **CDRRMO `552-3939`**, **BFP `160`**, your **nearest Police Station**, and your **family's preferred hospital**.
- **Identify your nearest evacuation center.** Ask your barangay hall — every barangay has a designated primary site.
- **Pack a go-bag**: 3 days of water/food, IDs in a waterproof bag, prescription medicines, flashlight, power bank, cash, change of clothes.
- **Know your hazard exposure** — open the **[Smart Map](/command-center)** → toggle the **PHIVOLCS Tsunami** layer if you live on the coast, or the **Hazard Maps** group for flood/landslide/storm-surge zones near your home.
- **Subscribe to PAGASA + PHIVOLCS** social media and turn on push notifications.
