# Emergency Response Procedures & Evacuation Routes — General Santos City

What to do **during** an earthquake, tsunami, fire, or flood in General Santos City — plus where evacuation routes typically lead and how to reach official rescue services in the moment.

> **In any life-threatening emergency, call [911](tel:911) immediately.**
> For a full hotline directory (CDRRMO, BFP, Police stations, hospitals), see **[Emergency Hotlines & Early Warning Systems](/services/disaster-preparedness/access-disaster-preparedness-information-and-early-warning-systems)**.

---

## Earthquake

GenSan sits in a seismically active region — the Cotabato Trench and several active fault systems are nearby.

### During shaking — Drop, Cover, Hold On

1. **DROP** to your hands and knees before the shaking knocks you down.
2. **COVER** your head and neck with one arm. Get under a sturdy desk or table if one is nearby; otherwise crawl next to an interior wall away from windows.
3. **HOLD ON** until the shaking stops — be ready to move with your shelter if it shifts.

**Avoid:** doorways (not safer than other interior spots in modern buildings), elevators, exterior walls with windows, and stairwells during shaking.

### After shaking

- Check yourself and others for injuries; provide first aid if able.
- Expect **aftershocks** — they can be nearly as strong as the main shock for up to several days.
- If you smell gas, hear hissing, or see broken electrical lines: leave the area, shut off the main if you can do so safely, and call **BFP `160`** or **CDRRMO `552-3939`**.
- **If you are within 2 km of the coast and the earthquake felt strong or lasted more than 30 seconds, move to higher ground immediately** — see the Tsunami section below.

## Tsunami (coastal General Santos)

Tsunami inundation zones for GenSan are modeled by PHIVOLCS — view them as a layer on the **[Smart Map](/command-center)** (PHIVOLCS Hazards → Tsunami Inundation).

### Natural warning signs (you may have only minutes)

- Strong or long earthquake near the coast.
- Loud roar from the sea (sounds like a freight train or jet).
- The sea **suddenly receding far past the normal low tide line**.

### What to do

1. **Move inland and uphill immediately** — do **not** wait for an official siren or text alert. Local tsunamis can arrive in 5–20 minutes.
2. Target high ground at least **2 km inland** or **10 m above sea level**, whichever you can reach first.
3. Stay on high ground until **CDRRMO** or **PHIVOLCS** issues an "all clear." A tsunami is a series of waves — the first is not always the largest.

**Coastal barangays with tsunami exposure:** Tambler, Buayan, Bula, Calumpang, Dadiangas East/South/West, City Heights, Labangal, Baluan, Ligaya. Check the Smart Map for your specific block.

## Fire

1. **Get out and stay out.** Do not retrieve belongings. Close doors behind you to slow the spread.
2. **Crawl low under smoke** — air is cleanest near the floor.
3. **Feel doors before opening** — if hot, find another exit.
4. **Once safely outside, call [160](tel:160)** (BFP GenSan) or **[911](tel:911)**. Then call your nearest **Police Station** — see the directory.
5. If your clothing catches fire: **Stop, Drop, and Roll**.

> **Apartment / high-rise:** use stairs, never the elevator. If trapped, seal door gaps with wet cloth, signal from a window, and call BFP with your exact location.

## Flood

GenSan flood-risk maps (5-year, 25-year, 100-year return periods) are on the **[Smart Map → Hazard Maps](/command-center)** (Project NOAH).

### Before water rises

- Move valuables, electronics, and chemicals to upper floors.
- Unplug appliances; switch off mains if water is expected indoors.
- Fill containers with safe drinking water.
- Check on neighbors, especially elderly residents and those with mobility issues.

### During the flood

- **Do not walk or drive through floodwater.** Six inches (15 cm) can knock you off your feet; 30 cm can float a vehicle. Floodwater often hides open manholes, debris, downed power lines.
- Move to the highest level of your home; **do not enter the attic** unless it has roof access — you can be trapped by rising water.
- Call **CDRRMO `552-3939`** or **`0943-461-4548`** if you need rescue. State your **exact address and the number of people** with you.

### After the flood

- Avoid contact with floodwater — assume it is contaminated with sewage, fuel, and chemicals.
- Do not turn power back on until an electrician confirms the wiring is dry.
- Throw out food and medicine that came in contact with floodwater.

## Storm surge (typhoon-driven coastal flooding)

PAGASA storm-surge advisories use four levels (SSA1–SSA4). View the modeled extent for each level on **[Smart Map → Hazard Maps](/command-center)** (Project NOAH storm-surge layers).

- **SSA1 (2–3 m)** — Stay alert, prepare go-bag, monitor PAGASA.
- **SSA2 (3–4 m)** — Pre-evacuate low-lying coastal areas.
- **SSA3 (4–5 m)** — Mandatory evacuation of coastal communities.
- **SSA4 (>5 m)** — Catastrophic; evacuate to the farthest inland evacuation center.

When a tropical cyclone is forecast to enter the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR) and head toward Mindanao, CDRRMO activates pre-emptive evacuation orders for the most exposed coastal sitios.

## Evacuation centers — how to find yours

Each of GenSan's **26 barangays** has at least one designated primary evacuation center. The most common host sites are:

- **Barangay halls** and adjacent multi-purpose covered courts
- **Public elementary and high schools** (when classes are suspended)
- **City-run gymnasiums** in larger barangays (Calumpang, Lagao, Labangal, San Isidro, Fatima)

### To find your assigned center

1. Call your **barangay hall** — they maintain the official assignment list.
2. During an active emergency, **CDRRMO** at **`552-3939`** can confirm the nearest open center and the safest route there.
3. Volunteers from **Delta 9** (City Radio Communication Services, **`554-0474`**) coordinate evacuation logistics by HF/VHF radio during power outages — useful when mobile networks are down.

---

## When mobile networks fail

- **Listen** to AM/FM radio for **PAGASA** and **NDRRMC** bulletins. Battery-powered or hand-crank radios are part of every go-bag.
- **Delta 9** ([554-0474](tel:+6383554047400)) operates the city's emergency radio net and can relay messages.
- **SMS often gets through when calls do not** — short text uses far less bandwidth than voice.
