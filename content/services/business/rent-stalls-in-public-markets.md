Find out how to apply for a market stall in one of General Santos City's public markets.

## Who Can Apply

- Filipino citizens at least 18 years old
- Registered business owners (DTI for sole proprietors, SEC for partnerships/corporations, CDA for cooperatives)
- Applicants with no existing delinquent market rental accounts with the city

---

## Requirements

- **Unified Application Form** (from the City Market Administrator's Office or BPLO) [TO BE VERIFIED — confirm issuing office]
- **DTI/SEC/CDA** Certificate of Registration
- **BIR** Certificate of Registration (Form 2303)
- **Barangay Clearance** (business)
- **Mayor's/Business Permit** (or proof of application for new businesses)
- **Community Tax Certificate (CTC)**
- **Valid government-issued ID** (with photo)
- **2x2 ID photos** (recent)
- **Sanitary Permit** (from City Health Office, especially for food vendors)
- **Fire Safety Inspection Certificate (FSIC)** if applicable

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Inquire about available stalls**
   - Visit the **City Market Administrator's Office** [TO BE VERIFIED — confirm office name and location] or check announcements on [gensantos.gov.ph](https://gensantos.gov.ph/).
   - Ask about available stall types (wet market, dry goods, cooked food, fish/seafood, etc.), sizes, and monthly rental rates.

2. **Submit application**
   - Complete the application form and attach all required documents.
   - Indicate your preferred market, stall type, and product line.

3. **Evaluation and approval**
   - The office reviews your application for completeness and eligibility.
   - Stall assignments may be subject to availability, waitlists, and priority criteria set by city ordinance.

4. **Sign the lease/rental agreement**
   - Upon approval, sign the **Market Stall Lease Agreement** with the City Government.
   - Review terms: rental rate, payment schedule, rules of conduct, grounds for termination.

5. **Pay initial fees**
   - Pay the required **rental deposit**, **advance rent**, and **other fees** at the City Treasurer's Office.
   - Secure your **Official Receipts**.

6. **Occupy and operate**
   - Set up your stall per market rules (signage, sanitation, waste disposal, fire safety).
   - Display your **Mayor's/Business Permit** and **Sanitary Permit** visibly.

## Rental Rates and Fees

- Rates vary by **market location**, **stall size**, and **type of goods** sold.
- Monthly rental fees are set by **city ordinance** [TO BE VERIFIED — confirm current rate schedule].
- Additional charges may apply for **utilities** (electricity, water) and **common area maintenance**.

---

## Rules and Obligations

- **Pay rent on time** — delinquent accounts may result in stall forfeiture.
- **Maintain cleanliness** and comply with **sanitary standards**.
- **No subleasing** or unauthorized transfer of stall rights.
- **Renew permits annually** (Mayor's/Business Permit, Sanitary Permit, FSIC).
- **Follow market hours** and operational guidelines set by the City Market Administrator.
- **Cooperate with inspections** from health, fire, and market management teams.

---

## Practical Tips

- Visit the market in person to see available stalls and assess foot traffic before applying.
- Prepare all documents in advance — incomplete applications cause delays.
- Keep copies of your lease agreement and all receipts.
- For **fish and seafood vendors**: GenSan's fishing industry provides strong supply chains — coordinate with the Fish Port Complex for sourcing.
- Join the **market vendors' association** (if one exists) for collective advocacy and updates.
